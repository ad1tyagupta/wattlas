# GRID//SCOPE · provisional visual system

> Status: approved direction, provisional identity. No external brand assets are required or implied.

## Positioning

GRID//SCOPE is an analytical operating surface for European energy-infrastructure opportunity. It should feel authoritative, calm, technical, evidence-led, and spatial—not militaristic, theatrical, or vendor-specific.

## Huashu position questions

- **Narrative role:** a daily analytical workspace whose hero is the map; evidence and score explanations are supporting acts.
- **Viewing distance:** approximately one metre on a laptop or desktop display. Dense information is acceptable when hierarchy remains clear.
- **Visual temperature:** cool, calm, precise, and slightly editorial. Amber and rust appear only when the data earns urgency.
- **Capacity:** the map owns roughly two-thirds of the viewport. Controls and evidence progressively disclose around it rather than covering it with equal-weight cards.

## Core identity

- **Wordmark:** `GRID//SCOPE`, typeset rather than illustrated. The double slash is the signature divider used sparingly in labels and loading states.
- **Primary dark:** `#07100F` — mineral-black water and application ground.
- **Raised surface:** `#0D1918` — inspectors and utility rails.
- **Land:** `#142321` — neutral map landmass.
- **Line:** `#29413D` — boundaries and separators.
- **Text:** `#E1EBE8` — primary content.
- **Muted text:** `#849993` — metadata.
- **Signal mint:** `#72D9BD` — selection, verified state, and available capacity.
- **Demand amber:** `#E2B45C` — high Infrastructure Demand.
- **Risk rust:** `#D66F5F` — System Risk and data conflicts.
- **Context periwinkle:** `#8FAEFF` — supporting generation/storage context.

The palette is functional. No decorative rainbow gradients or extra accent colors are permitted.

## Typography

- **Display and interface:** IBM Plex Sans Condensed, chosen for industrial authority and efficient information density.
- **Body:** IBM Plex Sans.
- **Data and metadata:** IBM Plex Mono.
- Minimum interface body size: 14px. Primary inspector content: 15–16px. Click targets: at least 44×44px.

## Layout grammar

- Desktop shell: 48px command bar, 220–260px layer rail, fluid map, 340–400px inspector, and a 56px timeline.
- Use plain separators and tonal shifts rather than nested rounded cards.
- Radius is limited to 2–6px for controls and popovers; the map and primary panels remain rectilinear.
- A single selected region creates the strongest visual contrast. Unselected data recedes.
- The detailed evidence dossier may scroll; the map and time control remain spatially stable.

## Map grammar

- Neutral cartography with thin administrative and power-system boundaries.
- The active lens changes the choropleth, legend, and inspector language together.
- Region polygons carry normalized score color. Projects use capacity-scaled points. Grid nodes and corridors appear only at appropriate zoom levels.
- Hover gives a terse preview; click locks selection and opens evidence. No tooltip should carry a complete dossier.
- Movement is purposeful: one camera transition on selection, one inspector reveal, and restrained timeline interpolation.

## Data-status grammar

- `daily refreshed` replaces the ambiguous word `live`.
- Source states are `current`, `cached`, `stale`, `failed`, and `not configured`.
- Estimated and inherited values use patterned fills or explicit text, never merely a slightly different color.
- A failed update never erases the last-known-good snapshot.

## Anti-patterns

- No Palantir logo, trade dress, or proprietary imagery.
- No generic neon-blue cyber aesthetic, purple gradients, glass-card stack, decorative radar sweeps, filler statistics, emoji, or ornamental icons.
- No fabricated project facts or scores.
- No 3D globe in the first working version.

## Signature detail

The 120% detail is the evidence trace: selecting any score contribution should visually connect raw value, normalization rule, confidence, date, and source without losing the map selection.
